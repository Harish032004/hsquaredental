What are Dental Implants?

A permanent, structural replacement for missing teeth

Dental implants are medical-grade titanium posts that are surgically positioned into the jawbone beneath your gums. Once in place, they allow your dentist to mount replacement teeth or a bridge onto them, acting just like natural roots.

Biocompatible Titanium

Structural Integrity

Multiple Missing Teeth

Benefits of Dental Implants

The closest thing to having your natural teeth back

Lifetime Durability

Unlike bridges or dentures, implants are designed to last a lifetime with proper care.

Prevents Bone Loss

Stimulates the jawbone to prevent the bone shrinkage that occurs with missing teeth.

Improved Speech

No more worrying about slipping dentures causing slurred speech or mumbling.

Eat Anything

Functions like a natural tooth, allowing you to eat hard and crunchy foods with ease.

No Cavities

Implant materials cannot decay, though healthy gums are still essential for support.

Natural Aesthetics

Seamlessly blends with your surrounding teeth for a perfect, confident smile.

Caring for Your Implants

Keep your restoration healthy and stable

Daily Cleaning

Brush and floss exactly like natural teeth. Use an interdental brush if needed.

Healthy Habits

Avoid smoking, as it can interfere with bone healing and implant stability.

Professional Care

Regular dental cleanings to monitor the health of the gum and bone around the implant.

Night Protection

If you grind your teeth, a night guard is essential to protect the ceramic crown.

Pro Tip

Implants don't get cavities, but they can get gum disease. Keep those gums clean!
